# Differ

## One-sentence description
Differ is a platform for experiential computing: it takes a machine-interpretable definition of an experience and returns visualizations that surface where a context-aware experience might break for particular populations or settings.

## The original motivation
Grew out of Northwestern's Design, Technology, and Research course inside Delta Lab, then continued for almost two years. Built on the idea that the same context-aware experience can mean something different depending on who encounters it and where.

## The problem being explored
How to connect how a person expresses a concept to how a machine interprets it, aimed at designers working on inclusive, context-aware systems — before shipping.

## Shirley's role
First author on the CHI 2026 submission presenting Differ alongside real-world case studies, with Jiachen He and Prof. Haoqi Zhang (Delta Lab, Northwestern). Dates listed: Dec 2023 – Sep 2025.

## Important technical decisions
- Four core abstractions: concept expressions, accountable perspectives, issues of concern, and visualizations.
- Accountable perspectives capture meaning differences across geography (urban/rural, state, neighborhood), demographics (age, budget, wheelchair accessibility), and setting (time of day).
- Issue functions score a perspective for prevalence, conceptual fit, popularity, safety, and affordability.
- Reference systems combine Yelp and Foursquare data, public crime datasets, neighborhood data, and census data; visualizations with geopandas and Plotly.
- Case studies use real-world reference systems (e.g. Chicago neighborhoods) rather than synthetic data.

## Important visual or experiential decisions
- Portfolio presents Differ as an instrument rather than a flat case-study card template.
- Visualizations are the product surface for designers poking at where assumptions break.

## What changed during development
Public story notes the project took as long as it did largely because real neighborhoods are harder to render responsibly than toy datasets.
